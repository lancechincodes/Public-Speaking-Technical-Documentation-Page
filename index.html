5 Keys to Effective Public Speaking

Created by Lance Chin

Body Movement
Ask yourself: Am I fidgeting?

1st Key: Use body movement as an extension of your voice.

  • Use your hands to emphasize parts of your speech. Be careful to avoid overusing them as they can create a distraction.
  • Try the diamond method. You will be able to give attention to everyone in the crowd.
  • Avoid repetitive movements. Remember the focus is your message, not your movements!

Breathing
Ask yourself: Am I relaxed?

2nd Key: Be mindful of your breathing.

  • Close your mouth after every sentence to avoid saying "um".
  • Take deep breathes before speaking.
  • Check your posture - your feet should be shoulder-width apart, weight equally distributed, and arms down to your sides.

Focus
Ask yourself: Am I hitting my main points?

3rd Key: Focus on the purpose of your speech.

  • If possible, form an outline of your speech before you speak.
  • Be mindful of your audience.
  • Keep your rhetoric simple.

Project Confidence
Ask yourself: Am I speaking loudly and clearly?

4th Key: Project confidence whether you are pretending or not.

  • Watch experienced speakers and learn from them.
  • Realize that you are given the floor for a reason - people want to listen to you.
  • The more often you pretend to be confident,the more comfortable you will become with being confident!

Practice
Ask yourself: Am I prepared?

5th Key: Trust your practice.

  • Ask people you trust for constructive feedback.
  • Practice in front of a mirror.
  • Record yourself and listen to yourself. Listening to your own voice might sound weird at first, but you'll get used to it!
